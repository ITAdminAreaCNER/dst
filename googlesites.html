Guide: Google Sites for Service

Creating a simple, no-cost website for your Area, District, or Group.

Why Use Google Sites?

No Cost & No Ads

A professional-looking website with no hosting fees or advertisements.

Incredibly Simple

If you can make a document, you can build a website. No coding required.

Integrates Everything

Easily embed Docs, Sheets, Calendars, Maps, and Forms.

Mobile Friendly

Your site will automatically look great on phones, tablets, and computers.

A Critical Note on Website Ownership

To ensure your committee's website is preserved for future trusted servants, it must be created inside your committee's Shared Drive. A site created in a personal "My Drive" is owned by that individual. If they leave service, the website and its address can be lost forever, breaking a key link to the public and service structure.

Common Service Use Cases

1. Public Website for an Area, District, or Group

This is the digital front door to help the still-suffering alcoholic find us. It can provide essential information to the public and our members.

  • Key Pages: Home, Find a Meeting (embed a Google Sheet or link to a meeting list), About AA, Events (embed a Google Calendar), Contact Us (embed a Google Form).
  • Benefit: A simple, up-to-date website is one of the most effective ways to carry the message in the digital age.

2. Internal Hub for a Committee

A committee can create a private, unlisted website that serves as a central resource for its members. This site would not be public and only accessible to those with the link.

  • Key Pages: Roster (list of members), Meeting Documents (embed a folder from your Shared Drive containing minutes and agendas), Calendar (embed the committee's Google Calendar), Key Resources.
  • Benefit: Organizes all of a committee's important information in one easy-to-navigate place, making service more efficient.

3. Best Practices for Google Sites

  • Create it in a Shared Drive: Always start by navigating to your committee's Shared Drive and clicking `+ New > More > Google Sites`. This ensures the group owns the site.
  • Start with a Plan: Before you build, sketch out the pages you need. What is the most important information a visitor needs to find?
  • Anonymity is Paramount: Do not use photos where individuals are identifiable. Use first names and last initials only for service positions. Never post members' personal contact information on a public site.
  • Keep it Updated: A website with outdated information is not helpful. The committee responsible for the site must review it regularly to ensure meeting lists, event calendars, and contact information are current.
  • Use the "Insert" Menu: The power of Google Sites is on the right-hand "Insert" menu. Use it to directly embed content from your Google Drive, Calendars, and Maps. This ensures that when you update the source (like a meeting list in Google Sheets), the website updates automatically.
