Why We Use a Paid Workspace

Professional Identity

A custom domain (e.g., chair@area99.org) provides legitimacy and clarity, reinforcing that the position, not the person, is communicating.

Perpetual Ownership

Assets like Shared Drives and Calendars are owned by the organization. When a member rotates out, the assets remain secure, ensuring continuity.

Central Administration

The Area IT Admin can manage users and enforce security policies, providing a layer of protection impossible with only personal Gmail accounts.

True Collaboration

Shared Drives ensure files belong to the team, not an individual. This prevents accidental data loss when members rotate out of their positions.

A Hybrid Approach: Workspace & Personal Accounts

A Workspace account is not required for every member. The system is designed for Workspace and personal Gmail accounts to work together seamlessly.

Role of Workspace Accounts

Held by a few trusted servants (e.g., Chair, Secretary, PI/PA Coordinators, IT Admin).

  • Create and own central assets.
  • Manage members of Shared Drives.
  • Send official communications from a custom domain (e.g., pipa@district9aa.au).
  • Delegate management permissions.

Role of Personal Gmail Accounts

Used by the majority of committee members and trusted servants.

  • Receive invitations to collaborate.
  • View and edit files in Shared Drives.
  • Participate in shared calendars.
  • Caveats:
    • Risk of lost passwords can lock you out of service work.
    • Communications come from personal addresses (e.g. surferdude87@gmail.com) which can seem unprofessional.
