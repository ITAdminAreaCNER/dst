Guide: Google My Maps for Outreach

Use a shared map to coordinate outreach, track history, and see where your efforts are needed most.

Why Use a Shared Map?

Creates a living history of your committee's work.

Prevents duplicating efforts when members rotate.

Helps you strategically plan your outreach efforts.

A Critical Note on Map Ownership

To ensure your committee's outreach map is preserved for future trusted servants, it must be created inside your committee's Shared Drive. A map created in a personal "My Drive" is owned by the individual. If that person leaves service, the map and all its valuable history can be lost forever.

1. Create the Map (The Right Way)

  1. Navigate to your committee's Shared Drive in Google Drive.
  2. Click the + New button in the top-left.
  3. Hover over More, then select Google My Maps from the menu.
  4. The map is now created and saved securely in the Shared Drive.
  5. Click on the map title ("Untitled map") to give it a clear name (e.g., `District 10 PI/CPC Outreach Map`).

2. Organize with Layers

Use layers to categorize your outreach targets.

  1. Rename the "Untitled layer" to something specific (e.g., `Hospitals & Clinics`).
  2. Click Add layer to create more categories (`Schools`, `Libraries`, etc.).

3. Add & Detail Locations (The Important Part!)

This turns the map into a powerful record-keeping tool.

  1. Select the correct layer (e.g., `Hospitals & Clinics`).
  2. Use the search bar to find a location and click + Add to map.

5. Share with Your Committee

Because the map is in the Shared Drive, anyone who is a member of that drive can already find and edit it. You can also click the Share button to get a direct link to send to members for convenience.
