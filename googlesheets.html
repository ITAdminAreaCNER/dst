Guide: Google Sheets for Service Coordination

A powerful tool for tracking finances, motions, outreach, and more.

Why Use Google Sheets?

Centralized Data

All your information is in one shared, accessible place.

Real-Time Updates

Multiple members can view and edit the sheet simultaneously.

Structured Info

The grid format is perfect for organizing lists and data clearly.

Historical Record

Creates a lasting, searchable record for future trusted servants.

A Critical Note on Sheet & Data Ownership

To ensure the service entity retains control over its information, any Google Sheet used for service (treasury, motions, contact lists) must be created inside a Shared Drive. A sheet created in a personal "My Drive" is owned by the individual. If that person leaves service, the data can be lost forever.

4. Best Practices for Sheets

  • One Sheet, One Purpose: Create separate Google Sheets files for Treasury, Committee Motions, etc. Don't put everything in one file.
  • Use Templates: Create a blank "master" version for each task. When you need a new one, just make a copy.
  • Keep Formatting Consistent: Use clear headers and don't merge cells unnecessarily, as it can make sorting and filtering difficult.
  • Protect Important Data: For things like treasury reports, you can protect cells or entire sheets to prevent accidental changes. Right-click a sheet tab or a cell and look for "Protect range".
