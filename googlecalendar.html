A Complete Guide to Shared Google Calendars

Using Google Calendars to keep your Area, District, Group, or Committee organized.

Why Use a Shared Calendar?

Single Source of Truth

Everyone sees the same schedule, preventing confusion.

Improves Transparency

All members can see upcoming events and deadlines.

Reduces Confusion

Avoids conflicting dates and double-booking events.

Separates Service/Personal

Keeps service commitments off your personal calendar.

A Critical Note on Calendar Ownership

The person who creates a calendar is its permanent owner. To ensure continuity, it is strongly recommended that a central Workspace account creates the calendar. If a personal Gmail account creates the calendar and that person leaves, the Area IT Admin cannot recover, transfer, or preserve the calendar. It will be lost forever.

A Special Note for Online Meetings (Google Meet)

For an online meeting, the features (like meeting duration and participant limits) are determined by the account of the event organizer, not the calendar owner.

  • A meeting organized by a free Gmail account is limited to 60 minutes.
  • A meeting organized by a paid Workspace account has much higher limits (e.g., 24 hours).
  • You can transfer ownership of a single event to a Workspace user to "upgrade" its meeting features. This does not change who owns the calendar.

How to Set Up & Manage a Shared Calendar

1. How to Create the Calendar

The designated person (ideally the IT Admin or a central Workspace account) should follow these steps.

  1. Open Google Calendar on a computer.
  2. On the left menu, find "Other calendars" and click the plus sign (+).
  3. Select "Create new calendar" from the menu.
  4. Give it a clear, consistent name (e.g., `District 10 Events Committee` or `Area 99 PI/PA Calendar`).
  5. Add a brief description of its purpose.
  6. Click the "Create calendar" button.

2. How to Share the Calendar

  1. Find the new calendar in the left menu. Hover over it and click the three dots (â‹®) for options.
  2. Select "Settings and sharing".
  3. Scroll to "Share with specific people or groups" and click "+ Add people and groups".
  4. Enter the email addresses of your committee members (both Workspace and personal Gmail users).
  5. Choose the correct permission level for them from the dropdown menu (see below).
  6. Click Send to invite them. They will receive an email to add the calendar.

3. Understanding Permissions

Giving the right level of access is key to effective collaboration.

See all event details

Best for general members. They can stay informed but cannot add or change anything.

Make changes to events

Ideal for trusted servants who need to schedule meetings or update event details.

Make changes AND manage sharing

This is the highest level of control. If the IT Admin created the calendar, they should grant this permission to the committee chair or a designated tech steward. This allows that person to manage the calendar and add/remove members without needing the admin for every change.

4. Best Practices for Use

  • Use Clear Event Titles: Instead of "Meeting," use "District 10 Monthly Committee Meeting."
  • Add Details: Put the location, video call link, and agenda in the event description.
  • Add All Relevant Dates: Use it for meetings, workshops, project deadlines, and events.
  • Review Regularly: The chair should review the calendar at each meeting to ensure it's up-to-date.
