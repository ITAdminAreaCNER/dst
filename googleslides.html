Guide: Google Slides for Presentations

For workshops, orientations, and explaining service concepts visually.

Why Use Google Slides?

Visual Storytelling

Explain complex ideas with simple text, images, and diagrams.

Consistency

Create templates to ensure all service presentations have a unified look.

Reusable Content

Easily copy and update presentations for the next workshop or service term.

Presenter Tools

Use speaker notes to guide you through your presentation without cluttering the slides.

A Critical Note on Presentation Ownership

To ensure valuable resources like workshops and orientations are preserved, any presentation for service must be created inside a Shared Drive. A presentation created in a personal "My Drive" is owned by the individual. If they leave service, that resource can be lost forever.

Common Service Use Cases

1. Orientations for Service Roles

When a new GSR, DCM, or Committee Chair is elected, a simple presentation can help them understand their role. This ensures knowledge is passed on consistently.

  • Example Slides: "What is a DCM?", "Key Responsibilities," "Who to Contact for Help," "Important Dates."
  • Benefit: A new trusted servant gets the same foundational information every time, improving continuity of service.

2. Workshop & Committee Presentations

Whether it's a District workshop on the Traditions or a PI/PA committee presenting its annual plan, Google Slides helps structure the information clearly and professionally.

  • Keep it Simple: Each slide should have one main idea. Use bullet points, not long paragraphs.
  • Visualize Data: Instead of a table of numbers, use a simple chart (which you can create in Google Sheets and insert into Slides).

3. Best Practices for Google Slides

  • Use a Master Template: A designated service member can create a master presentation with the Area's color scheme and logo. Everyone can then make a copy to ensure a consistent look and feel for all official presentations.
  • Less is More: The slides are for your audience, not for you. Keep them clean and simple. Put your detailed script and reminders in the "Speaker notes" section below each slide, which only you can see while presenting.
  • Store in Shared Drives: Like all other service documents, presentations must be stored in the appropriate Shared Drive folder (e.g., `03 - Committee Projects/Workshops`). This preserves them as a resource for future members.
  • Collaborate on Content: Before a presentation, share the deck with other committee members and give them "Commenter" access. They can add feedback and suggestions in the margins without altering the slides directly.
