Session 1: Digital Tools for AA Service

Increasing our effectiveness by applying spiritual principles in a digital age.

The Problem We All Know

The Old Way

Vital information (minutes, reports) lives on personal laptops. If a trusted servant is absent or rotates, the information is lost, and the group conscience is uninformed.

The New Way

Service information belongs to the group, not the individual. It's live, transparent, and accessible, ensuring continuity and a fully informed group conscience.

The "Why": Our Spiritual Principles

Tradition One: Common Welfare

A unified digital system promotes our common welfare. When service information is central and secure, we reduce friction and are better able to serve our primary purpose.

Tradition Two: Trusted Servants

Shared access to reports and agendas ensures the group conscience is fully informed, empowering the group to guide our "trusted servants."

Tradition Four: Autonomy

Separate Shared Drives for each Group or District creates a technical firewall, enforcing the autonomy that is essential to A.A.

Tradition Seven: Self-Supporting

This applies to our information. When records are in a group-owned space, the group is "self-supporting" with its own history, independent of any one member.

The "Why": Our Spiritual Principles

Tradition Nine: Least Organization

This guides us to collect the least possible information required for service, reminding us to be disciplined and purposeful in what data we store.

Tradition Eleven: Attraction

Our public-facing websites and documents should be dignified and informative, never promotional, based on attraction rather than promotion.

Tradition Twelve: Anonymity

These tools help us manage anonymity responsibly, with secure internal spaces for service work and public-facing documents that protect personal anonymity.

Concept IV: Participation

Sharing a draft agenda in Google Docs allows every member to contribute their thoughts before a meeting, leading to a more engaged group conscience.

The "Why": Our Spiritual Principles

Concept V: Right of Appeal

An accessible digital record upholds this right. A clear, searchable history of minutes provides a transparent record of all past decisions.

Concept IX: Leadership

Preserving service history gives new trusted servants the context of past decisions, allowing them to provide "sound and well-thought-out" leadership from day one.

Warranty Two: Prudent Reserve

A treasurer maintaining a live budget in a Google Sheet gives the committee a constant, clear view of its finances, supporting responsible decisions.

Warranty Six: No Unqualified Authority

The digital principle of "least privilege" ensures no single person has unqualified authority over all information. Access is a shared responsibility.

The Core Solution: Understanding Shared Drives

The single most important concept is storing files so they belong to the position, not the person.

👤

My Drive (Personal)

Like a personal filing cabinet. If you move, it goes with you.

🤝

Shared Drive (Group)

Like the office filing cabinet. The files belong to the position.

When we create service documents in a Shared Drive, we solve the rotation problem.

Toolkit in Action: Running the Business

Transparent Finances

Problem: The treasurer's report is a mystery until the meeting.

Solution: A live budget in Google Sheets. The committee can view it anytime, ensuring financial decisions are based on current, clear information.

Accessible Decisions

Problem: "What did we decide about that last year?"

Solution: Use Google Docs for minutes and a Google Sheet to track all motions. This creates a permanent, searchable group memory.

Clear Action Items

Problem: "Who was supposed to do that? What's the status?"

Solution: Add columns to the motion tracker Sheet to assign members to action items and track their completion. Everyone knows who is doing what.

Effective Meetings

Problem: Agendas and reports aren't sent out in time.

Solution: Create agendas in Google Docs within the Shared Drive. Members can see the agenda develop and come to the meeting prepared.

Toolkit in Action: The "Easy Button"

How do we make it easy for members to access info and contribute without navigating complex Google Drives?

Simplify Data Entry

Use Google Forms as the simple front door. A member can fill out a form to submit a report or expense claim, and the data populates a Sheet automatically. No spreadsheet skills needed!

Simplify Access

Use Google Sites to create a simple dashboard. Instead of hunting through folders, members visit one webpage with clear links to the current agenda, budget, or minutes.

Toolkit in Action: Passing It On

How does a new trusted servant get up to speed quickly and feel supported?

Orientations

Use Google Slides to create a simple orientation presentation that can be improved and passed down year after year, ensuring continuity of knowledge.

Reference Library

Use Google Sites as a permanent home for service resources. New members have one place to find key documents, pamphlets, training info, and past presentations.

Explanation of Pricing

Standard Pricing

  • Business Standard: $264 per user, per year. This is the main plan needed for features like Shared Drives.
  • Under the current system, only one license is needed as long as everyone else uses a personal Gmail account to collaborate.
  • If other service bodies (Districts, Groups) want custom email addresses, they must purchase their own licenses at this price.

Alternative Options

  • Split Licensing: A cheaper "Business Starter" plan ($130/year) is available, but this requires going through an Australian Google Workspace Reseller and a minimum of 20 users.
  • Not-For-Profit (NFP): If the Area becomes an Incorporated Association and gains NFP status, pricing drops dramatically to $0/year for Business Starter and $66/year for Business Standard.

For more details, see the official Google Workspace Pricing page and NFP Eligibility requirements.

Session 2: Q&A

Let's open the floor for questions and discussion.

Q&A: Common Questions

"This sounds too complicated."

"That's a common concern. The goal is to start simple. We can use tools like Google Forms so that most members never have to touch a spreadsheet. The website has step-by-step guides, and we're here to help each other. It's about progressing towards perfection."

"What about security & anonymity?"

"That's our highest priority. The 'Spiritual Principles' page on the website goes into this in detail. Shared Drives give us more control than personal accounts, allowing us to manage exactly who sees what, in line with our Traditions and Concepts."

"Why Google? Does this cost money?"

"Great question. GSO and the General Service Board already use these tools. It's also a very low-cost model. The Area can pay for a few core accounts, but most members can participate fully with their free, personal Gmail accounts, keeping us self-supporting."

Q&A: Common Questions (Continued)

"Why not just a free Gmail account?"

A free account is owned by an individual. If that person leaves or loses the password, we lose everything. A Workspace account is owned by the Area, ensuring perpetual ownership and continuity of service.

"Who owns the Workspace account?"

The account should be owned by the Area, not one person. Best practice is using an Australian Google Workspace Reseller so the Area pays directly and multiple trusted servants are listed as contacts. This prevents a single point of failure and upholds group autonomy.

"Can the IT admin see everything?"

Yes, which is why they must be a "Trusted Tech Servant." To provide accountability, an IT Subcommittee should be formed to act as a group conscience, ensuring the admin's role is guided by spiritual principles and group oversight.

Q&A: Zoom vs. Google Meet

Cost & Self-Support

Zoom Pro costs ~$267/year for meetings over 40 minutes. Google Meet, included with Workspace, has no time limits for business meetings. Service bodies can save money by using the Area-provided Meet.

Value & Ownership

For the same price as Zoom Pro, a Group could get a full Workspace account. This provides Meet plus all the other tools (Drives, Docs) and solves the problem of accounts being tied to personal emails, ensuring continuity.

Ease of Use

Google Meet is extremely easy to use. It runs directly in a web browser on any computer, and doesn't require guests to install any special software on their Windows, Mac, iOS, or Android devices.

Conclusion & What's Next

By placing our service information in a central, group-owned Shared Drive, we are not just organizing files. We are living our Traditions, Concepts, and Warranties.
