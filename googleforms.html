Guide: Google Forms for Service

For surveys, event sign-ups, and collecting information easily.

Why Use Google Forms?

Easy Data Collection

Create simple forms to gather information in a structured format.

Protects Anonymity

Forms can be set to not collect email addresses, respecting our traditions.

Automatic Summaries

Instantly see responses summarized with charts and graphs.

Links to Sheets

Automatically send all responses to a Google Sheet for easy tracking.

A Critical Note on Form & Data Ownership

To ensure the service entity retains control over its information, the Google Form and the Google Sheet it sends responses to must be created inside a Shared Drive. If created in a personal drive, the data belongs to the individual, and can be lost when that person rotates out of their position.

Common Service Use Cases

1. Surveys & Information Gathering

Use a form to collect information from groups or members, such as updating meeting details, gathering GSR reports before an assembly, or surveying the fellowship on a specific topic.

  • Example: A District could send a form to all its GSRs asking for updated meeting times, addresses, and whether the meeting is open or closed.
  • Benefit: All the information is collected in one place (a Google Sheet) instead of across dozens of separate emails.

2. Event Registration & RSVPs

Easily manage sign-ups for service workshops, area events, or committee functions. You can ask for a first name and last initial, what group they're from, and if they need any materials.

  • Example: An Events Committee can create a form to handle registrations for a local convention or roundup.
  • Benefit: Provides an accurate headcount and a simple way to organize volunteer sign-ups for the event.

3. "Contact Us" & Help Forms

A form on an Area or District website can guide newcomers or members of the public to the right person. Questions can help route the inquiry automatically.

  • Example: A question like "What is your inquiry about?" could have options like "Finding a Meeting," "Speaking to an Alcoholic," or "Public Information."
  • Benefit: The form can be set to notify the correct trusted servant (e.g., the Helpline chair or PI/PA chair) based on the response, ensuring a quick and appropriate reply.

4. Best Practices for Google Forms

  • Create it in a Shared Drive: Always start by navigating to your committee's Shared Drive and clicking `+ New > Google Forms`. This ensures the group owns the form and its data.
  • Check Your Settings: In the "Settings" tab of your form, make sure to **uncheck** "Collect email addresses" to protect anonymity where needed.
  • Link to a Sheet: In the "Responses" tab, click the Google Sheets icon to create a new spreadsheet where all responses will be automatically logged. Ensure this sheet is also saved in the Shared Drive.
  • Keep it Short & Simple: Only ask for the information you absolutely need. The shorter the form, the more likely people are to complete it.
