Understanding Shared Drives for Service

A central hub for your service body's files and collaborative work.

What is a Shared Drive?

Unlike your personal "My Drive," where you own the files, files in a Shared Drive belong to the service body as a whole. This means that if a trusted servant rotates out of a position, the files remain in the Shared Drive for the next person. It provides a stable, centralized, and secure location for all service-related documents and resources.

Key Functions in Service

Group Ownership

Files are owned by the service body, preventing data loss when trusted servants rotate.

Managed Access

Control who can view, comment, or edit files based on their service role.

Centralized Information

All members can find service files in one place, creating an informed group conscience.

Protecting Anonymity

Administrators can control sharing to protect sensitive information and member anonymity.

What Can It Contain?

A Shared Drive is a versatile storage space that can hold a wide variety of file types for service work. This includes native Google Workspace applications and much more.

Google Workspace Files

  • Google Docs: For agendas, minutes, reports, and flyers.
  • Google Sheets: For budgets, outreach lists, and motions trackers.
  • Google Slides: For orientations and workshop presentations.
  • Google Forms: For surveys, event sign-ups, and collecting information.
  • Google Sites: For creating simple websites for a committee or district.
  • Google Vids: For creating informational videos.

Other Service-Related Files

  • Images: Photos from service events or for public information.
  • Videos: Recordings of workshops or presentations (with consent).
  • Audio Files: MP3s of service-related talks or workshops.
  • Documents: PDFs, Microsoft Office files (Word, Excel, PowerPoint).
  • And more: Archives (.zip) of past service records.

Access From Any Device

You can access your service files from virtually any device, ensuring the information you need is always within reach.

Desktop (Windows & macOS)

By installing Google Drive for desktop, you can access Shared Drive files directly from your computer's file system, just like any local file.

Mobile (Android & iOS)

The Google Drive app gives you full access to your Shared Drives on your phone or tablet, so you can stay productive in service from anywhere.

Sharing Files with a Link

For non-sensitive documents intended for the public (like an event flyer), you can generate a shareable link. You can set the permission to "Anyone with the link," which allows anyone to access the file without a Google account.

It's crucial to set the access level appropriately. For read-only access, Viewer is ideal. If you want to gather public feedback, you can use the Commenter role.

Note: Your ability to share files and the specific options available are dependent on the security permissions granted by your service body's administrator and your role within the Shared Drive.

Carrying the Message More Effectively

We encourage trusted servants to consider how these tools can improve their service. Could a shared budget in Google Sheets provide more transparency for your committee? Could a shared folder of PI flyers help your district coordinate outreach? By embracing these tools, we can reduce friction, improve collaboration, and better focus on our primary purpose.

Key Takeaway

A Shared Drive is an essential tool for service, providing a secure, permanent, and organized home for a service body's files. It simplifies rotation, ensures continuity, and offers seamless access across all your devices.
