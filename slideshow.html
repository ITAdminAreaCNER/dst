Digital Tools for AA Service

Increasing Our Effectiveness Through Spiritual Principles

Are we being *effective*?

The Old Way: Information is Siloed

The secretary is sick, the treasurer is on holiday. Where are the minutes and financial reports?

On personal laptops, unavailable.

The group conscience stalls, our service is less effective, and when rotation happens, valuable history is lost.

The New Way: Information is Shared

Information belongs to the position, not the person. It's stored in a central, group-owned space.

Anyone who needs it can access it.

Our service becomes more effective, transparent, and continuous.

The 'Why': Our Spiritual Principles

The Traditions

Tradition 1 (Common Welfare): Unified information promotes unity.
Tradition 2 (Trusted Servants): An informed group conscience can guide its leaders.
Tradition 7 (Self-Supporting): A group must own its own history and records.
Tradition 12 (Anonymity): Manage internal and public information responsibly.

The Concepts

Concept IV (Participation): Shared access to drafts and agendas encourages participation.
Concept V (Right of Appeal): A clear, searchable history of motions ensures fairness.
Concept IX (Leadership): Give new leaders the history they need to be effective from day one.

The Warranties

Warranty II (Prudent Reserve): Live, shared budgets provide financial transparency.
Warranty VI (No Unqualified Authority): Digital "least privilege" ensures no one has total control over all data.

The Core Solution: Shared Drives

The most important concept is moving from personal ownership to group ownership.

"My Drive"

Like a personal filing cabinet at home. If you move, the files go with you. Information gets lost.

"Shared Drive"

Like the filing cabinet at the GSO. The files belong to the office. Information is preserved.

The 'How': The Digital Toolkit in Action

Running the Business of A.A.

Problem: Can't find motions, action items are forgotten.

Solution: Use `Docs` for minutes and `Sheets` for a live, searchable tracker of all motions and decisions.

The "Easy Button" for Communication

Problem: Members struggle with technology.

Solution: Use `Forms` to easily collect information (agenda items, contact updates, expense claims) without anyone needing to touch a spreadsheet.

Ensuring Continuity

Problem: A new trusted servant starts with zero information.

Solution: Use `Slides` to create a simple orientation that can be passed down, preserving valuable knowledge.

Conclusion & What's Next

By placing our service information in a central, group-owned **Shared Drive**, we live our Traditions, empower the group conscience, and become truly self-supporting with our own information.

Q & A

Common Questions

"This sounds too complicated. I'm not good with computers."

That's a common and valid concern. The goal is to start simple. We can use tools like Google Forms as an "easy button" so most members never have to touch a spreadsheet. The website has step-by-step guides, and we're here to help each other. It's about progress, not perfection.

"What about security and anonymity?"

This is our highest priority. Shared Drives actually give us more control than personal accounts. We can manage exactly who sees what, in line with our Traditions. The "Spiritual Principles" page on the website goes into this in detail, but this system is designed to protect our members and our fellowship.

"Why Google? And does this cost money?"

1. Following GSO's Lead: The General Service Office and Board use these same tools. We're aligning with the practices of A.A. as a whole.
2. Low Cost & Self-Supporting: The Area pays for a few core accounts. Most members participate for free. This keeps us self-supporting without a large financial burden.
3. Group Autonomy: This is a framework, not a mandate. Each group or district is free to choose the tools that best serve their own needs.
