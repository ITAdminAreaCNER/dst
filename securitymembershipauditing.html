Security & Membership Auditing

Ensuring our digital spaces remain secure, relevant, and accountable through regular audits.

Operating Procedure: Security & Membership Auditing

Regular audits are a spiritual responsibility, ensuring our digital spaces remain secure and relevant. This responsibility is shared between local drive managers and the Area IT Administrator.

Local Responsibility: Quarterly Membership Review

The Manager of each Shared Drive (e.g., the DCM,Committee Chair or GSR) is responsible for reviewing their drive's member list at least once per quarter, and always after an election or service rotation. This review ensures that:

  • Outgoing members have been removed.
  • New members have been added with the correct permission level ("least privilege").
  • The member list accurately reflects the current composition of the service body.

Area Responsibility: Annual High-Level Audit

Once a year, the Area IT Administrator should conduct a high-level audit of the entire Workspace. This audit does not involve looking at files or content. Its purpose is purely structural and technical:

  • Confirm Managers: Ensure every Shared Drive has at least one active "Manager." If a drive's manager has rotated out without assigning a new one, the IT Admin can intervene to assign a new manager based on direction from the Area, District or Group.
  • Identify Abandoned Drives: Look for drives that appear to have no members or have been inactive for a very long time, to be flagged for archival or deletion by the Area Committee.
  • Review Global Settings: Check the overall Workspace security settings to ensure no overly permissive sharing policies have been enabled.
  • Report to Area: The IT Administrator should provide a simple report to the Area Committee summarizing these technical findings, ensuring transparency and accountability for their role.
