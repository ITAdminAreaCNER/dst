Guide: Google Meet for Service Meetings

A no-cost alternative for online meetings, workshops, and seminars.

Why Use Google Meet?

Fully Integrated

Works seamlessly with Google Calendar for easy scheduling and notifications.

Secure & Private

Meetings are encrypted, and host controls help keep your meeting safe.

Accessible

A Critical Note on Meeting Features

A meeting's features (duration, participant limits) are tied to the account of the event organizer. To get premium features, a trusted servant with a paid Workspace account must be the final owner of the calendar event.

  • Free Gmail User: Meetings are limited to 60 minutes and 100 participants.
  • Workspace User: Meeting limits are much higher (e.g., 24 hours), ensuring your service meeting can proceed without interruption.

How to Schedule a Meeting with Premium Features

There are two ways to ensure your meeting has the extended time and participant limits.

Option 1: Workspace User Creates the Event (Recommended)

The simplest method. A trusted servant with a Workspace account creates the event on the shared calendar.

  1. Open the shared committee's Google Calendar.
  2. Click on a time slot to create a new event.
  3. Add a title (e.g., "District 10 Monthly Meeting").
  4. Click the blue button that says "Add Google Meet video conferencing".
  5. Invite guests. Click Save. The meeting will automatically have premium features.

Option 2: Transfer Ownership to a Workspace User

If a member with a free Gmail account creates the event, they can transfer ownership to a Workspace user to upgrade the meeting's features.

  1. Creation: The free Gmail user creates the event on the shared calendar and invites the paid Workspace user as a guest. At this point, the meeting has a 60-minute limit.
  2. Transfer: The Gmail user (the current owner) opens the event in their Google Calendar and clicks the **More actions** menu (the three vertical dots).
  3. Select "Change owner" from the menu.
  4. Enter the email address of the paid Workspace user and click "Change Owner".
  5. Acceptance: An email is sent to the Workspace user. They must click the link in the email to accept the ownership transfer.
  6. Result: Once accepted, the Workspace user becomes the new organizer, and the Google Meet link is automatically upgraded with their premium features.

Best Practices for Online Meetings

  • Respect Anonymity: Remind members they can turn off their video. Encourage the use of first names and last initials for display names. Avoid recording meetings unless there is a specific group conscience to do so for a workshop.
  • Use Host Controls: The meeting creator (host) has special controls. Click the shield icon ("Host controls") to manage who can share their screen or send chat messages.
  • Manage Participants: The host can mute participants who may have forgotten to mute themselves, helping the meeting run smoothly.
  • Use the Chat for Motions: Having the secretary type motions into the chat box allows everyone to see the exact wording before a vote, which prevents confusion.
